our petition to amend the "Skills Visa Act" H.R.2131 sponsored by Rep. Darrell Issa [R-CA49]

petition for these edits to "Skills Visa Act" H.R.2131 SEC. 102. IMMIGRANT VISAS FOR ENTREPRENEURS (a)(ii) as follows:

  • (I) TREATY INVESTORS TRACK ONE: An alien who has been issued a visa or otherwise provided nonimmigrant status under section 101(a)(15)(E)(ii) (not including alien employees of the treaty investor) who have maintained that status for a minimum of 5 years and have benefited the United States economy and either created full-time employment for not fewer than 3 United States workers for a minimum of 5 years or made an investment of no less than $250,000 which money has been irrevocably committed to the enterprise, and is at risk should the business fail.

  • (II) TREATY INVESTORS TRACK TWO (LONG-TERM E2 TREATY INVESTORS): An alien who--
    (A) has been issued a visa or otherwise provided nonimmigrant status under section 101(a)(15)(E)(ii) (not including alien employees of the treaty investor) who have maintained that status for a minimum of 5 years and
    (B) has been lawfully present in the United States in a status that allows for employment authorization for a continuous period, not counting brief, casual, and innocent absences, of not less than 10 years.

(we will notify you via mailing list as soon as petition is ready - we welcome your suggestions for amendments!)

On Thursday, June 27, 2013 the House of Representatives Committee on the Judiciary discussed H.R. 2131 for purposes of markup and move that the committee report the bill favorably to the House and also refferred to the following section of the proposed bill:

Original Passage
SEC. 102. IMMIGRANT VISAS FOR ENTREPRENEURS (a)(ii)

(ii) TREATY INVESTORS -- Immigrants who have been issued a visa or otherwise provided nonimmigrant status under section 101(a)(15)(E)(ii) (not including alien employees of the treaty investor) who have maintained that status for a minimum of 10 years and have benefitted the United States economy and created full-time employment for not fewer than 5 United States workers for a minimum of 10 years.
